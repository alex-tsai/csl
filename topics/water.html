Harvest their own water

Rain, snow and the soil provide plants the water they need. Rain provides all the water the CSL uses other than that used for drinking and handwashing, which is not permissible by law.

Green Roof

The 3-acre site around you puts roughly 3.25 million gallons to use each year. The green alone captured nearly 90% of rainwater that fell on it.

Interior

All of the water that leaves the building, whether from sinks, drains or toilets, is cleaned on site and reused as flush water.

Landscape

The porous soils, pervious surfaces and numerous raingardens of the site allow rainwater to soak into the ground and support plant life or recharge groundwater.
